## Project Overview
This Corporate Performance Dashboard is a centralized data visualization tool that aggregates critical business metrics. It helps management monitor:

Financial Metrics: Tracking EBIT, Revenue, Expenses, and Cash Reserves.

Operational Efficiency: Monitoring expenditure by department and project-based capital spending (CAPEX).

Human Capital: Visualizing headcount (FTEs), overtime hours, leave balances, and gender equality ratios.

Sales Performance: Comparing current year (CY) vs. prior year (PY) sales quantities and growth trends.

## Dashboard Layout & Features
### 1. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
The top ribbon displays real-time snapshots of vital stats:

Financials: Cash Reserves (2.3m) and Stock Price (17.43, up 3.0%).

Project Pipeline: 11 New Projects and 10 Community Projects.

Marketing & Social: 44 Social Campaigns and 4 Alliances.

### 2. Financial Analysis
Rolling Chart: A dual-axis chart showing actual spend (bars) versus budget (line) across the fiscal year.

Sales Growth v % Profit: A scatter plot categorizing business units (Hardware, Clothing, IT, etc.) based on profitability and growth.

Expenditure by Dept: A detailed table comparing PY and CY spending across Operations, Strategy, Finance, Commercial, and HR.

### 3. Human Resources & Diversity
Workforce Distribution: Breakdown of FTEs and Overtime hours across major locations (London, Manchester, Liverpool, Bristol).

Gender Equality: Donut charts showing the percentage of female employees in IT, HR, and Operations.

Hires vs. Terminations: A column chart tracking staff turnover trends month-over-month.

### 4. Operations & Control
Control Issues: Visualizing PY vs. CY issues in IT, HR, and Operations to monitor risk management.

Leave Balances: Highlighting the top 5 employees with the highest leave balances to manage liability.

## Data Visualization Style
The dashboard utilizes a clean, professional color palette (corporate reds, blues, and greys) with high-contrast charts for readability. It leverages:

Interactive Filters: Ability to filter data by region (e.g., England) and metric type.

Time-Series Tracking: Visualizing cycles through the "Sales Quantity CY v PY" line graph.
